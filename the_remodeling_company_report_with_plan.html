What we'd actually build

System 1 · Hero

Lookalike Demand Engine + Light CRM

The growth lever for the firm and the answer to your magic-wand: "I need to find in some focused way how to find customers like my customers." Two integrated pieces. The light CRM holds your past-client roster and every new lead, tagged with the attributes that matter (location, project type, source, contract value, conversion outcome) – which means you also get the kind of reporting you used to be able to run. The demand engine sits on top: profiling for true lookalikes, AI-enabled outreach through the channels that actually work for high-end residential remodeling, and a repeating motion that compounds as the review base grows and the audience definition sharpens with more data.

What we're building

  • Light CRM stood up with your past-client roster cleaned and loaded, tagged by project type (kitchen, bath, addition, whole-home), location, source, contract amount, and outcome – plus a simple intake for every new lead going forward
  • Reporting layer that produces the cuts you described in the walkthrough: total contract amount of all jobs in city A, conversion rate by lead source, project-type distribution year-over-year, anything else worth tracking
  • Attribute-level lookalike profiling on the past-client base – not just demographics, but the trigger events, employers, life stages, neighborhoods, and project patterns that define who your real customers are
  • Demand engine in motion: targeted outreach into the lookalike audience using AI-enabled research (LinkedIn, professional directories, industry and employer associations, neighborhood data – your doctors-at-a-specific-hospital example fits exactly here), automated review and referral capture on every completed project, and Google Business Profile / Houzz Pro discipline tuned to your real service footprint
  • The engine is built to run. Setting up the engine is the build; running campaigns through it is the ongoing work. The 60-day operational ramp is included – Crane operates the engine alongside your team, ships real campaigns, and tunes the system against actual results. After the ramp, Crane can keep running it as the fractional growth team owning the data and marketing work behind your demand, or your team can take the keys when ready.

Why it matters

  • Your stated reality: close to 100% of current pipeline is past-clients and referrals. The firm has 28 years of credibility and visible public proof of it – and that credibility isn't currently being converted into demand on purpose.
  • Lookalike-based outreach is structurally different from the broad-spend marketing approaches you've tried before. It targets people who actually look like your customers – not a broader audience you hope contains some.
  • The new owner inherits a demand engine, not just a referral list. This is the single most concrete piece of value you can leave behind on the demand side.

What it replaces

  • The current Excel-based lead tracker (a sheet plus a linked sheet that produces "kind of crude" reports, in your words)
  • Single-channel acquisition (word-of-mouth and referrals only) with no system catching new reviews as they post
  • The implicit hope that referrals will keep flowing through the transition without intervention

What "AI Readiness" actually means

AI Readiness isn't about whether you use ChatGPT. It's about whether the work you actually do is set up to benefit from AI – so it saves you time, raises the quality your team produces, and gives you back the hours for the leadership and people work only you can do.

Five conditions matter:

  • Your data is usable. AI can't work with information that lives only in someone's head.
  • You know where your time is going. Repetitive tasks you can name are tasks you can delegate.
  • You know your customers and community. Who they are, what they need, and how they find you.
  • There's a clear way you do things. AI amplifies what's repeatable. If your methods live in one person's head, there's nothing to amplify yet.
  • You have momentum. You've tried things, you're curious, and you want to move.
